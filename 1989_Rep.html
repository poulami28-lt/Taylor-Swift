1989 & Reputation

In 2014, Swift moved to New York City and released her fifth album, 1989, her first official pop record. It debuted with 1.28 million US sales and produced three number-one hits: "Shake It Off", "Blank Space", and "Bad Blood". The 1989 World Tour became the year’s highest-grossing tour. Swift criticized streaming platforms over artist royalties, prompting Apple Music to change its policy. She became the first woman to win Album of the Year twice at the Grammys. From 2015–2016, she dated Calvin Harris and co-wrote “This Is What You Came For.” Public feuds with Kanye West and Kim Kardashian led to her being "cancelled" online.In 2017, she countersued DJ David Mueller for sexual assault and won. These events shaped her sixth album, Reputation, which explored fame and public perception. It sold 1.21 million copies in its first week and topped charts globally. The Reputation Stadium Tour became the highest-grossing North American tour ever.
